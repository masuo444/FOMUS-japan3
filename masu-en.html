What is Masu

A Japanese vessel for meaning

Masu has been used in Japan for over 1,300 years. Born as a measuring tool, it became a symbol of celebration, prosperity, and new beginnings. Linked to the word “masu” (to increase), it still conveys people’s wishes today.

Craft rooted in materials

Design value from the material.

FOMUS Masu is made from offcuts of Japanese hinoki used in architecture. We never cut trees specifically for masu. We reframe leftover wood from home construction into vessels with new value.

Hinoki offers deodorizing and antibacterial properties, plus a calming scent from phytoncides. Even the wood shavings are reused as packaging, so you can enjoy the aroma when opening.

FOMUS Masu is naturally sustainable.
